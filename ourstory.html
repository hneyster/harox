Boulder, Colorado


In May 2018, Harold and Roxanna connected on Bumble. Both of us had recently been in relationships and weren't looking for anything serious [Roxanna: we were looking to have hot girl summers]. Roxanna swiped right because she had just finished reading Born to Run and was fascinated by the concept of ultrarunners. Roxanna’s compassion and adventurousness piqued Harold’s interest.
A couple of screenshots taken by Roxanna and sent to her sister, Hayley while on FaceTime during this era.
After a short conversation in which Harold impressed Roxanna by knowing where Goa was, and Roxanna showed her mastery of grammar, Harold asked Roxanna if she would like to join him for a picnic that following day. Roxanna, being new to Bumble and not wanting to commit to an entire meal, counter-offered a walk to Wreck beach, just off the coast of the University of British Columbia campus where they were both pursuing graduate school. That next day, Roxanna was extremely close to canceling, but her friends Fiona and Caroline convinced her that she should go and, if it didn’t go well, they would call her and fake a lab emergency. Fortunately, no lab emergencies emerged, and that first date led to 3 more hangouts in the next 2 weeks, including Harold cooking seitan for Roxanna at her apartment. Harold then left for the rest of the summer & headed off to do field work in CO, and they spent their summer texting all day every day. During these long conversations, they discussed their enthusiasm for adventures, science, and much more, and the possibility of joining each other on adventures when Harold returned.


August 18/19 — Tomyhoi Lake, North Cascades, WA
Harold returned, but they weren’t exactly sure where they stood. But they were excited about adventuring together regardless. Our first backpacking trip together. As soon as Harold arrived back from his summer field work, he planned a blueberry-filled backpacking trip to Tomyhoi Lake and let Roxanna invite her labmates: Tom and Caroline. This was the first of many adventures. At first they didn’t see any blueberries, and Harold worried that his credibility was ruined, but just around the bend they found the bushes thick with them. Roxanna was in awe of Harold’s enthusiasm and never ending amounts of energy. At one point Harold was carrying both their backpacks while not missing a beat in the conversation. Harold was also amazed when Roxanna began casually running down the mountain with her heavy backpack.

June 2020 — The Break Up
We broke up in June 2020. While the previous two years had been filled with adventure and camaraderie, we had struggled to create community and a relationship where we both felt supported. During our break up period, Roxanna learned how to lead multi-pitch climbs, went on an overnight kayaking trip to Granite Falls, and spent a lot of time summitting peaks. Harold replicated hundreds of bird surveys that were first conducted decades ago, getting up at 4ma to bike to various parts of the city and record birds, and spent time diving into neglected pursuits and also visited Yukon.

June 2021 — We go to therapy
Harold received a postdoc job offer in Vermont, and as his time in Vancouver was coming to an end. We recognized that we would soon be living on opposite sides of the continent. Was there a possibility of making our relationship work? Time was running out. Shania had recently gone to couples therapy, and recommended her therapist, Allison. In June, we went to our first session and were amazed. We learned that many ideas that seemed inevitable were not. We realized that we both had the agency to show up and grow our relationship towards something joyful. Instead of blaming each other, we began to seize the opportunities that our conflicts could provide for growth. We left that first session believing in a fulfilling future together.
